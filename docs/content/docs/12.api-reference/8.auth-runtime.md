Use this entrypoint for:

- Better Auth to Convex bridge setup with `defineAuth(...)`
- actor and guard definitions for protected app handlers
- small auth/authorization helpers like `requireAuth(...)` and `ensureTenant(...)`

Do not use this entrypoint for client auth state inside Vue components. For that, use [Composables](/docs/api-reference/composables).

## Main APIs

### `defineAuth(...)`

Use `defineAuth(...)` in Convex auth setup to connect Better Auth, user sync, and Trellis bootstrap plumbing.

Reach for this when:

- your app uses Better Auth
- you want Trellis to own the Convex-side auth bridge
- you need the supported `custom` escape hatch for auth-centric Better Auth configuration

Do not treat `defineAuth(...)` as your business authorization layer. Identity belongs here; tenant roles and app permissions belong in your app model and guards.

### `defineActor`

Use `defineActor.fromAuth()` or `defineActor.fromMembership(...)` to derive the app actor from the authenticated identity and app data model.

This is the bridge between transport identity and business role. Keep it narrow and app-owned.

### Guard and auth helpers

- `defineGuard(...)`, `open`, `and(...)`, `or(...)`, `can(...)`
- `requireAuth(...)`
- `ensureTenant(...)`
- `loadTenantResource(...)`
- `deny(...)`

Use these helpers to express permission checks and resource ownership in protected handlers. They are intentionally small and should stay close to your app rules rather than becoming a second framework.

## Recommended Flow

1. `defineAuth(...)` wires identity
2. `defineActor(...)` maps identity to your app actor
3. `defineGuard(...)` expresses allowed actions
4. `createApp(...)` registers protected handlers with those rules
